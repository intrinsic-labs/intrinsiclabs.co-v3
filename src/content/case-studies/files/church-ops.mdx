### The Problem
Christ Covenant Church in Matthews, NC needed two things: a way for their congregation to explore their global missions program interactively, and a way to track attendance for new member classes (previously managed with paper for 85+ attendees per class).

Commercial solutions existed but were expensive, inflexible, and couldn't be customized to the church's specific vision and workflows.

### What I Built

**Global Missions Kiosk:**
A native Android app (Kotlin, Jetpack Compose) deployed on two Elo 4304L 43" interactive touchscreens managed via EloView. Displays an interactive map of missionaries with profiles, prayer requests, and real-time updates. Content is backed by Airtable so church staff can update missionary information without developer involvement.

I managed the full lifecycle: hardware procurement, EloView device provisioning and configuration, app development, deployment, and ongoing maintenance.

**Check Right In:**
Started as a free project and turned into paid work. An attendance tracking app (Swift/SwiftUI) that operates across a network of iPads, centralizing all attendance data in one location. Originally backed by Airtable, I later migrated the system to Supabase and built a custom admin dashboard with Vite so the client could still manage everything themselves without developer involvement. Replaced paper tracking for 85+ person classes, eliminating human error and enabling historical data retention.

Both apps follow the same principle: **build self-sustaining systems.** The client manages their own data through simple interfaces (Admin dashboard, EloView) without needing to call me.

### Stack
**Kiosk:** Kotlin, Jetpack Compose, Airtable, Elo 4304L hardware, EloView MDM
**Check-In:** Swift, SwiftUI, Supabase, Vite (admin dashboard)
**Hardware:** 2x Elo 4304L 43" Interactive Displays with Android backpacks

### Status
Both deployed and in active use. 1,000+ people have used the check-in system over 4+ years. Kiosks have been running continuously since deployment.
